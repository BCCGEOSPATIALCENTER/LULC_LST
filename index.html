Key Findings

🏙️ Urban Heat Island Effect

Built-up areas average 44.6°C — nearly 12°C hotter than water bodies (32.8°C), confirming a strong Urban Heat Island effect in Round Rock, Texas.

🌿 Vegetation Cooling Role

Vegetation and Agriculture share the highest emissivity (0.98), efficiently radiating heat and maintaining lower surface temperatures than built-up zones.

🪨 Bare Soil Heat Risk

Bare soil reaches 42.4°C average — close to built-up areas — indicating undeveloped land is also a significant thermal stress zone in peak summer.

📊 Dominant Land Cover

Vegetation covers 57.2% of the classified area, yet built-up (16.5%) and bare soil (18.3%) together drive the high average temperatures across the scene.
